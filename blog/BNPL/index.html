BNPL: Start of a new era?

BNPL: Start of a new era?

  • October 13, 2022
  • Posted By Secys
  • Economy

BNPL is an acronym which stands for Buy Now Pay Later. It enables its users to purchase goods or services instantly and pay for them at a later stage in time. It is very much in trend these days and has become a buzz in many fintech companies which are working to add on new facilities to it. BNPL has recorded rapid growth at a CAGR of ~321 % by Gross merchandise value (total value of the loans disbursed to consumers), during FY’19 - FY’21.

But Still Why BNPL has gained such a word across the globe in such a small span of time?

BNPL has proliferated at a furious pace in the past couple of years, primarily due to two key reasons: relatively easy availability of credit for NTC customers (albeit with lower credit limits) and seamless user experience from onboarding to payments. For most users worldwide it serves as a low-cost, transparent, and frictionless financial product that enables lightning-fast credit access, hassle-free credit approval, disbursement, and tension-free repayment plans. In a country like India where the credit requirement is high, but access is limited to a few, it served as a revolution in the lending space where the credit requirements could be fulfilled digitally. The shift towards a digital payment space, following the pandemic, added the required fuel.

So has BNPL replaced traditional credit cards?

In recent times, credit card usage has started to limit, owing to its high cost of usage, opacity and requirement of credit history for issuing cards. Although BNPL still lags behind credit card payments and mobile wallets, it is important to note that the Various report highlights that the awareness of BNPL and its uses is rising, particularly among higher-income households, suggesting it is on the verge of becoming mainstream. 57% of the respondents to an Experian poll with a sample space across 6,000 consumers and 2,000 businesses indicated that BNPL might replace credit cards. BNPL user base is already at 1/3rd of credit card users.

What are the various ways in which different BNPL models operate?

  • App-based BNPL players, such as Lazypay, Simpl, Zest money, Cashe

  • E-Commerce/Travel Aggregators offering BNPL Services such as Amazon Pay Later (Capital Float), Flipkart Pay Later, and Ola Money Postpaid

  • Card Based on BNPL Players such as Postpe, Slice and Dhani Onefreedom Card

  • Mobile wallet players offering BNPL services such as Freecharge Pay Later, Paytm Postpaid, and Zip pay Later by Mobikwik

But still, are there any risks involved?

  1. Regulatory risk

    The recent report by RBI’s working committee on digital lending has several implications for BNPL players if implemented by the regulator. While it is a positive step in terms of regulating the product (lending to be done only through RBI-regulated entities, no FLDGs with non-NBFCs etc.), the near-term implications could be muted disbursal growth.

  2. Credit costs-risk models

    While the BNPL players are mostly focused on achieving scale in their ecosystem, risk management by keeping delinquencies under check remains one of the major impediments to achieving high profitability. With limited revenue drivers from the merchant side (MDR), the unit economics is yet to evolve completely.

  3. Slowdown in retail consumption

    India’s credit growth has been driven by the consumption sector for several years as the industry credit growth remained muted (lack of Capex cycle). Any slowdown in consumption growth could lead to lower consumer financing and, hence, impact the unit economics (and subsequently business viability) of the BNPL players in the near term.

So, summing up all BNPL has come in with its very own set of advantages and disadvantages full of fresh new potential for transforming the digital lending space and setting the tone for the start of the new era of credit. Now we have to just hop in the backseat and see where the transformation drives us.
